## Is Morocco Safe for Tourists in 2026?

Morocco is one of the safest countries in North Africa for tourists. The government has invested heavily in the Brigade Touristique (tourist police) in all major cities. Common sense and cultural awareness are your best tools.

### Emergency Numbers
- Police: 19
- Ambulance: 15
- Fire: 16
- Tourist Police: 177

### Safety Tips
- Use official licensed guides only
- Keep valuables secure in crowded areas
- Insist on taxi meters

## Solo Female Travel in Morocco

Morocco is increasingly popular with solo female travelers. Chefchaouen and Essaouira are particularly welcoming. Dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees), stay confident, and use MarocSphere's safety features for peace of mind.

## The Perfect 10-Day Morocco Itinerary

1. **Days 1-3:** Marrakech — Jemaa el-Fnaa, Bahia Palace, Majorelle Garden, souks
2. **Day 4:** Atlas Mountains — Tizi n'Tichka pass, Ait Benhaddou
3. **Days 5-6:** Sahara Desert — Camel trek, desert camp, sunrise
4. **Days 7-9:** Fes — Al-Qarawiyyin, tanneries, medina
5. **Day 10:** Chefchaouen — Blue streets, Spanish Mosque sunset

## Common Scams to Avoid

- Fake guides offering "free" tours
- Taxi drivers refusing to use meters
- "My uncle's shop" carpet pressure sales
- "Free" henna that demands payment
- Overpriced restaurants near Jemaa el-Fnaa

## Morocco Budget Guide

- **Budget:** $30-50/day (hostels, street food, buses)
- **Mid-range:** $80-150/day (riads, restaurants, taxis)
- **Luxury:** $200-500+/day (5-star riads, private guides)
