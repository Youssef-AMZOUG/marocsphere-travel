## Solo Female Travel in Morocco

Everything women need to know about solo travel in Morocco. Safety tips, what to wear, areas to avoid, and how to handle unwanted attention.

## Is Morocco Safe for Solo Female Travelers?

Yes, with preparation. Morocco is welcoming to female travelers but requires cultural awareness. The key is dressing modestly, staying confident, and using common sense.

## What to Wear

- Cover shoulders and knees in the medina
- Loose-fitting clothing is most comfortable and respectful
- A light scarf is useful for mosque visits and sun protection
- Swimwear is fine at hotel pools and private beaches

## Safety Tips

- Stay in well-reviewed riads in the medina (not isolated locations)
- Use MarocSphere's safety dashboard for real-time area ratings
- Hire official guides (government badge) in Fes medina
- Avoid walking alone in deserted areas after dark
- Say "La, shukran" (No, thank you) firmly to persistent sellers
- Keep your phone charged with emergency SOS enabled

## Safest Cities for Solo Women

1. **Chefchaouen** — Very safe, relaxed, popular with solo women
2. **Essaouira** — Laid-back, friendly, easy to navigate
3. **Rabat** — Modern capital, international feel
4. **Marrakech** — Safe in tourist areas, stay alert in deep medina
