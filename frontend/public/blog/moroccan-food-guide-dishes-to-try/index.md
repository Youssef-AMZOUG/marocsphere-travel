## 25 Moroccan Dishes You Must Try

From tagine to pastilla, discover authentic Moroccan cuisine. Best restaurants, street food spots, and dishes you cannot miss.

## Must-Try Dishes

1. **Tagine** — Slow-cooked stew in a conical clay pot. Chicken with preserved lemon and olives is the classic.
2. **Couscous** — Friday tradition. Steamed semolina with vegetables and meat.
3. **Pastilla (Bastilla)** — Flaky pastry with pigeon or chicken, almonds, and cinnamon. Fes specialty.
4. **Harira** — Tomato and lentil soup. The national comfort food, especially during Ramadan.
5. **Tanjia** — Marrakech's signature slow-cooked lamb in a clay urn.
6. **Mechoui** — Whole roasted lamb. Melt-in-your-mouth tender.
7. **Rfissa** — Shredded msemen with chicken and lentils in spiced broth.
8. **Zaalouk** — Smoky eggplant and tomato salad.
9. **Taktouka** — Roasted pepper and tomato salad.
10. **Briouats** — Crispy fried pastry triangles with meat or cheese.

## Street Food

11. **Msemen** — Flaky square pancakes. 2-3 MAD each.
12. **Sfenj** — Moroccan donuts. Best fresh from the fryer.
13. **Snail soup (Babbouche)** — A Marrakech specialty. 5 MAD per bowl at Jemaa el-Fnaa.
14. **Fresh orange juice** — 4 MAD per glass in the medina.
15. **Grilled sardines** — Essaouira's iconic dish.

## Drinks

16. **Mint tea** — "Moroccan whiskey." Poured from a height for froth.
17. **Avocado smoothie** — Unexpectedly popular street drink.
18. **Almond milk** — Traditional refreshment.

## Desserts

19. **Chebakia** — Honey-soaked sesame cookies.
20. **Sellou** — Nutty, spiced energy balls.
21. **Gazelle horns** — Crescent-shaped almond pastries.

## Regional Specialties

22. **Fish chermoula (Essaouira)** — Marinated grilled fish
23. **Tangia (Marrakech)** — Slow-cooked in communal oven
24. **Pastilla (Fes)** — The original, with pigeon
25. **Tafarnout bread (Atlas Mountains)** — Berber flatbread cooked in sand
