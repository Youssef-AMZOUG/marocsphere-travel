## Chefchaouen: Morocco's Blue Pearl

Why is Chefchaouen blue? How to get there, best things to do, photography spots, and where to stay in the Blue Pearl of Morocco.

## Why Is Chefchaouen Blue?

Multiple theories: Jewish tradition (blue represents the sky and heaven), practical mosquito repellent, or simply to attract tourists. Whatever the reason, the entire medina is painted in stunning shades of blue.

## Getting There

- **From Fes:** 4 hours by CTM bus (75 MAD) or grand taxi
- **From Tangier:** 3 hours by bus or shared taxi
- **From Marrakech:** Overnight bus or fly to Fes + bus

## Top Things to Do

1. Wander the blue-washed streets (the entire medina is photogenic)
2. Kasbah Museum (70 MAD) — fortress with garden and city views
3. Hike to the Spanish Mosque — best sunset viewpoint
4. Ras el-Maa waterfall — where locals wash clothes
5. Shopping — woven blankets, goat cheese, handmade soaps
6. Day trip to Akchour waterfalls (God's Bridge)

## Best Photography Spots

- The narrow streets of the old medina
- Blue-painted staircases with potted plants
- View from the Spanish Mosque at sunset
- Bab el-Ain entrance gate
- The Kasbah tower overlooking the medina

## Where to Stay

Budget: Dar Echchaouen (150-200 MAD). Mid-range: Casa Perleta (400-600 MAD). Luxury: Lina Ryad & Spa (1200+ MAD).
